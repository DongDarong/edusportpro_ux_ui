PLAYER SECTION - DETAILED DESCRIPTION (EDUSPORTPRO)

1. PLAYER SECTION PURPOSE
The Player section is the personal interaction layer of EduSportPro. It provides each athlete with visibility into their profile, team context, schedule, performance, and notifications.

Primary objective:
- Give players clear and timely access to information that affects their participation.
- Maintain a focused read-first experience with minimal administrative complexity.

2. PLAYER ROLE CAPABILITY SCOPE
Player capabilities are mostly read-only and personal-scope oriented.

Allowed (intended):
- View personal dashboard and status indicators.
- View own profile/information.
- View team information.
- View match schedule.
- View performance overview.
- View notifications.
- Access tournament/division information in read-only mode.

Restricted:
- No system-wide management (users, teams, divisions, tournaments, inventory).
- No event creation or broad audience notifications.
- No write authority on admin/coach operational modules.

3. PLAYER IA (INFORMATION ARCHITECTURE)
Player menu structure is designed for simple consumption:
- Dashboard
- Player Info
- Team Info
- Match Schedule
- Performance
- Tournament (read-only)
- Division (read-only)
- Notifications (top-nav access)
- Profile/Settings (top-nav access)

5. PLAYER WORKFLOW CHAINS (END-TO-END)

Workflow A: Daily Readiness
1) Open Dashboard.
2) Check notifications.
3) Review match schedule.
4) Confirm personal/team context.

Workflow B: Performance Follow-up
1) Open Performance page.
2) Review latest metrics.
3) Compare against upcoming schedule demands.

Workflow C: Competition Awareness
1) Open Tournament page.
2) Open Division page.
3) Understand standings and season trajectory.

6. NOTIFICATION & EVENT VISIBILITY LOGIC (PLAYER)
Player receives notifications from:
- Admin-created events (all teams or targeted team(s)).
- Coach-created events scoped to coach team.

Player behavior:
- Consume notifications and schedule data.
- No authority to broadcast or retarget communication.

7. ACCESS & PERMISSION MODEL (PLAYER VIEW)
Player permission intent:
- Read personal/shared visibility modules.
- No administrative or operational write actions.

Important boundary:
- Player cannot create calendar events.
- Player cannot modify tournament/division structures.

8. DATA MODEL RELATIONSHIP TO PLAYER MODULES
Primary entities consumed by player pages:
- users, roles, permissions
- players (personal profile)
- team_players, teams (membership and team context)
- matches, match_results (schedule/result visibility)
- performance records/statistics
- tournaments, divisions, groups, standings (read-only competition data)
- notifications, calendar_events, calendar_event_recipients

Key joins relevant to player scope:
- players.user_id -> users.id
- team_players.player_id -> players.id -> teams
- notifications.user_id -> users.id
- standings/team data for division pages

9. UI CHARACTERISTICS
- Responsive layout with sidebar and top bar.
- Consistent navigation pattern across player pages.
- Focus on clarity and low-complexity interactions.
- Read-heavy UX with limited form-heavy interactions.

10. QUALITY EXPECTATIONS FOR PLAYER MODULE
- Accurate personal data presentation.
- Timely schedule and notification updates.
- Strong permission boundaries (no hidden write paths).
- Consistent read-only treatment for tournament/division.

11. RECOMMENDED BACKEND ENFORCEMENT FOR PLAYER
- Authorize requests by user role and user-to-player mapping.
- Filter all data by player identity/team membership where needed.
- Deny all non-read actions on restricted modules.
- Add tests for:
  - player cannot create/update/delete admin or coach resources,
  - player can only access own personal profile data,
  - player receives only intended notifications.

12. SUMMARY
The Player section is a focused consumer interface in EduSportPro. It enables athletes to stay informed, prepared, and aligned with team operations while preserving strict role boundaries. Its design priority is simple, reliable visibility rather than management control.
